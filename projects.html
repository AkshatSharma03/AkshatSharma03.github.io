Current research at FactSpread

Social Value & Civic Messaging Evaluation Platform

FactSpread research platform
Grounded evidence Deterministic scoring Uncertainty analysis Auditability
  • As part of my work at FactSpread, I build a research platform for comparing civic topics and message drafts by expected public benefit alongside predicted engagement, using a value-of-information chain across reach, exposure, persuasion, evidence-aligned decision value, and measurability.
  • Ground candidate factual inputs in retained source documents and preserve source status, method, confidence, rationale, and uncertainty; language models assist with evidence extraction and review, but deterministic code computes the score.
  • Support sensitivity analysis, transparent pre-test persona simulation, expert and agent review, project history, batch comparison, experiment handoff, and exportable audit records while clearly labeling what still requires empirical validation.

Institutional AI & education

AI & Academic Writing: Student Survey Analysis

Boston University
Student AI use Writing pedagogy Survey analysis Faculty guidance
  • Analyzed 18 student survey responses about how undergraduates use AI during writing, including brainstorming, research, outlining, editing, fact-checking, and preserving individual voice.
  • Organized the findings around recurring AI roles, including researcher, architect, editor, and sparring partner, and student attitudes ranging from growth tool to overwhelmed backup or perceived threat.
  • Produced anonymized summaries and faculty-facing visualizations to support practical conversations about AI literacy, hallucination risk, academic integrity, and pedagogically appropriate use.
